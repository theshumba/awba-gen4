## Phase Goal

**As a** blind or keyboard-only learner working through a lesson and a timed review, **I want to** hear each verdict, noor gain and screen change in the app's calm voice, land my focus where the new screen begins, and feel which answer I picked without relying on colour, **so that** I can complete the whole flow with the same mercy — nothing silent, nothing lost, nothing red.

*(Derived from ROADMAP Phase-6 SC1/SC2/SC3 for the lesson + review runners. This plan wires the 06-04 primitives into AwbaLesson/AwbaReview and fills the two colour-only quiz gaps — mechanics byte-preserved.)*

<objective>
Harden the two runners at their authored seams: insert ONE composed `AW.announce` per resolve/answer (verdict + noor + combo folded), narrate the review timeout + the auto-skip + a single 10s warning, move focus to each reward/review screen's heading on every `innerHTML` swap (fixing focus-evaporation AND giving the SR the heading), give the quiz selection a non-colour channel (`aria-pressed` + a 2→3px border weight on the held paper-press), and replace the class-only/`pointer-events` "disabled" and resolved-option states with real `disabled` attributes so dead controls leave the tab order. Every number and timer is byte-preserved (ENG-03/04); the only new strings are aria/announce text. This flips the a11y-announce composed/10s/timeout/focus-to-heading and a11y-keyboard selection assertions from `{ todo }` to GREEN.

Purpose: ACC-02 announcements + ACC-01 keyboard operability + ACC-03 non-colour signal for the lesson/review flows — the "same mercy" bar for a blind/keyboard learner completing a lesson and a timed review.
Output: the announce/focus/aria-pressed/disabled insertions in `shared/awba-engine.js` (runners only).

Executor routing: **opus + `~/.claude/skills/emil-design-eng/SKILL.md` (read directly, no Skill tool)** — the selection cue is a NEW visible non-colour signal (visual judgement: the "pushed in and thicker" held-press + 3px weight must read as a genuine selection state without a new colour or a reflow, law 9 static). The announce/focus/disabled work is mechanical; opus does both. NEVER announce the same heading focus already reads (double-speak); NEVER pause or extend the timers.
</objective>
