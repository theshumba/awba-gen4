Sound is live (owner decision, 2026-07-16). The four cues below ship at shared/sfx/ and play in lessons, reviews and practice — free, public-domain (CC0) sounds from the Kenney “Interface Sounds” pack, levelled to Awba's laws: everything is quiet (peaks −12 dBFS), the wrong-answer cue is the lowest and quietest (−16 dBFS — a soft acknowledgement, never a buzzer), and nothing is music. The learner can silence all of it with More → Sound. To swap any cue, replace its file in shared/sfx/ and bump the service-worker cache version.

The live cues (shipping)

correct — a warm soft note (290 ms) · incorrect — a low quiet bong (120 ms) · complete — a fuller settle (490 ms) · streak — a gentle warm drop (190 ms)

Wood

A soft wooden tap: dry, close, unhurried.

String

A single muted pluck: warm, human, one note only.

Thump

A low warm body: felt more than heard, the quietest of the three.

The three families above are the earlier synthesized candidates, kept for reference and comparison — the shipped cues (top of the page) were chosen instead, from a free CC0 sound library, for their warmer real-world character. Swapping back to a synthesized family is a four-file replacement any time.

Every cue is a single dignified sound event — no melodies, no fanfares. All are normalized low (peak −12 dBFS; incorrect quieter and lower still, a soft downward acknowledgement, never a buzzer). Regenerate the synthesized set any time with node scripts/build-sound-candidates.js — the output is deterministic, byte-identical every run.
